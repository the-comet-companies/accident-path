## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Every competitor uses the same playbook: **phone number + web form + thin SEO pages**. None have invested in content marketing, interactive tools, or modern conversion funnels. The PI lead gen industry is a $3-5B market where leads cost $150-500 each, and our competitors are either burning cash on Google Ads or relying on dated SEO with no content depth.

**Our advantage:** We're the only platform building consumer-first value (education + tools) that earns organic traffic and converts through trust, not ad spend.

## 1. HOW EACH COMPETITOR ACQUIRES LEADS

### LegalMatch (legalmatch.com) — Our #1 SEO Competitor

| Channel | Strategy | Spend |
|---------|----------|-------|
| **Google Ads** | $500K-$1.5M/mo. Bids on "find a [type] lawyer" + "[practice] lawyer [city]" at $50-200+ CPC | Massive |
| **SEO** | 5,000-10,000+ pages, DA 60-70, ranks for 50K-100K keywords | Strong but shallow |
| **Phone** | Secondary to web form | Moderate |
| **Social** | Minimal engagement, burned community goodwill | Weak |
| **LSAs** | Cannot run (platform, not law firm) | N/A |

**Intake funnel:** 5-6 steps including a conversion-killing free-text "describe your issue" field. Dated UX. 1.8-star consumer satisfaction.

**Revenue model:** Attorneys pay $100-$2,000+/month subscriptions. Attorneys who pay more get more visibility → misaligned incentives.

**Vulnerability:** Shallow content, no tools, no Spanish, no E-E-A-T signals, terrible reviews, free-text intake kills mobile conversion.

## 5. LSA (LOCAL SERVICE ADS) — The Channel No LRS Can Use

Google Local Service Ads show "Google Screened" badges and appear above standard ads. They are the #1 trust signal in legal search.

**Critical finding: No LRS platform (including us) can run LSAs.** LSAs are for individual licensed attorneys/firms only.

**Implication:** Our partner attorneys can run their own LSAs. We should help them set up LSAs as part of our partner value proposition. This makes our partnership more valuable than just leads.

## 7. KEY COMPETITIVE INSIGHTS

### The 5 Things Every Competitor Gets Wrong

1. **No educational content.** Every competitor is a lead capture form with marketing copy. No guides, no "what to do after an accident," no injury explainers. Google rewards depth; users reward value.

2. **No interactive tools.** Zero competitors have calculators, quizzes, checklists, or journals. Our 10 tools are a category of one.

3. **Phone-first, form-second.** Most competitors optimize for phone calls. But 60%+ of accident victims research on mobile and prefer self-service intake before talking to someone.

4. **No post-intake value.** After form submission, the experience ends. No resources, no "while you wait" content, no follow-up tools. We keep users engaged.

5. **No trust architecture.** A single "State Bar Certified" badge is the universal trust signal. No attorney review dates, no transparent matching process, no author credentials.

### The 3 Things We Should Steal

1. **Attorney Search Network's affiliate program.** They're the only competitor with partner tracking infrastructure. We should build referral partnerships from Day 1.

2. **LawLinq's phone prominence.** Phone converts 2-3x better than web forms for PI. Our mobile bottom-bar CTA should have a prominent phone option.

3. **LegalMatch's keyword breadth.** They rank for 50K-100K keywords by having thousands of pages. Our content velocity plan (156 articles/year) will build toward this over time.
