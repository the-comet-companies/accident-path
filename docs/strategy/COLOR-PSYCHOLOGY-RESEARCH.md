# Color Psychology Research — AccidentPath.com

> Full research backing the design system color choices. See DESIGN-SYSTEM.md for the finalized palette.

## Research Summary

The palette was designed based on color psychology research targeting four emotions: **trust, authority, reliability, and care**. Users arrive after an accident — injured, scared, confused. The design must feel calming, trustworthy, and premium.

## Key Findings

### Trust → Blue (Deep Teal-Blue)
- Labrecque & Milne (2012): Blue hues consistently associated with competence and trustworthiness across cultures
- Singh (2006): Blue reduces physiological arousal (heart rate, skin conductance) — critical for stressed accident victims
- Elliot & Maier (2014): Cross-cultural association with safety, stability, reliability — holds across Western, East Asian, and Latin American populations

### Authority → Dark Values
- Darker blue tones perceived as more sophisticated and authoritative (Bottomley & Doyle, 2006)
- Near-black charcoals reinforce authority through visual weight

### Care → Warm Amber + Healing Green
- Valdez & Mehrabian (1994): Colors with moderate saturation and higher lightness = more pleasant, less threatening
- Lichtenfeld et al. (2012): Green enhances creative thinking and reduces anxiety — ideal for healing context
- Warm amber reads as approachable/optimistic without urgency of red-orange

### Cultural (Hispanic/Latino — 39% of CA)
- Madden et al. (2000): Blue perceived positively across all Latin American cultures. Warm tones (gold, amber, earth) culturally resonant in Mexican/Central American design. Red has mixed associations — restricted to errors only.
